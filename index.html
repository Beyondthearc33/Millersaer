Services

Two-Post Lifts
Two-post lifts are common in auto repair shops. During installation, it's crucial to ensure proper floor anchoring, correct lift placement, and adherence to manufacturer specifications for load capacity and safety features.
Four-Post Lifts
Four-post lifts are often used for vehicle storage and maintenance. Some models double as alignment racks, allowing for wheel alignment services. Proper leveling and securing of the lift are essential during installation.
Scissor Lifts
Scissor lifts provide a space-saving option. Installation involves careful consideration of the lift's weight capacity, proper hydraulic fluid levels, and electrical connections. It's crucial to follow the manufacturer's guidelines for regular maintenance.
In-Ground Lifts
In-ground lifts are recessed into the floor, providing a seamless workspace when not in use. Installation involves excavation, proper placement of lift components, and hydraulic system setup. Regular maintenance is essential to prevent issues with the hydraulic system.

Routine Inspection and Lubrication
Conduct routine visual inspections of your automotive lift. Check for any signs of wear, damage, or irregularities in the structure, hydraulic components, and safety features.

Keep all moving parts well-lubricated to reduce friction and prevent premature wear. Follow the manufacturer's recommendations for the type and frequency of lubrication.
Hydraulic System Maintenance
Regularly check hydraulic fluid levels according to the manufacturer's specifications. Ensure there are no leaks, and address any issues promptly to maintain the lift's hydraulic efficiency.

Inspect hydraulic hoses and fittings for wear, cracks, or leaks. Replace any damaged components immediately to prevent hydraulic system failures.
Safety Features and Controls
Test all safety features regularly, including locks, safety latches, and automatic shutoff mechanisms. Ensure they engage and disengage properly to guarantee a secure working environment.

Ensure that lift columns and arms are properly aligned. Misalignment can affect the lift's stability and performance. Adjust as necessary according to the manufacturer's guidelines.
Structural Integrity
Verify that the lift is securely anchored to the floor. Periodically check all bolts and fasteners for tightness, and retorque them as needed to maintain structural integrity.

Ensure that lift columns and arms are properly aligned. Misalignment can affect the lift's stability and performance. Adjust as necessary according to the manufacturer's guidelines.
Operator Training and Guidelines
Provide comprehensive training for lift operators. Emphasize safe operating practices, load limits, and emergency procedures to minimize the risk of accidents.

Always follow the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule and guidelines. Adhering to these specifications ensures warranty compliance and extends the life of your automotive lift.
